Pat Kenny: A flashback to Pat Kenny’s career in broadcasting

January 17, 2020January 17, 2020 Matthew MakelaMatthew Makela 0 Comments

Patrick Kenny is an accomplished Irish journalist, who now hosts on Virgin Media One the daily radio show The Pat Kenny Show for Newstalk and Pat Kenny Tonight’s current affairs series. He had a 41-year, high-profile career with RTÉ before all of this, in which he had been their highest-earning host for many years.

Kenny was a Television presenter on RTÉ’s Today Tonight, a Mid-1980s public affairs program. Having held the position of co-presenter of the 1988 Eurovision Song Contest, he went on an unusual path for a public affairs host. 

His Toy Show interview with actor Jerry Seinfeld was amongst the highlights of Kenny’s tenure while hosting The Late Late Show. Seinfeld remained tight-lipped, even as Pat Kenny dubbed him Jerry Sein-field. Kenny revealed on 27 March 2009 that he will leave at the end of the season as host of The Late Late Show.

The TV and radio host is building a massive steady income for himself and his family The Newstalk host has just released a comprehensive breakdown of results with an annual income of € 1,526,884 on his business, Pat Jenny media services.

Newstalk presenter has been a regular on-air and on our TVs for the last four decades, anchoring some of the highest-rated shows, including The Late Late Show, which he has been hosting for more than ten years. In 2013 Kenny left RTE and took a stance with Newstalk Radio. Previously Newstalk poached him to the radio network based on Dalkey in a € 2 m deal.

He also signed a broad deal with Virgin Media for his best-known show of public affairs, in which he discusses the nation’s problems. It is understood he signed a deal for € 500.00 with TV3 for the weekly series ahead of him.
